Battery-Free Voltage Sag Protection (EDLC): How It Works

A battery-free voltage sag protector corrects momentary voltage sags using EDLC ultra-capacitors instead of batteries. When the supply voltage briefly dips, the device injects compensating voltage within milliseconds, keeping sensitive equipment online — then draws no maintenance overhead because there are no batteries to replace. WESCO's TSP® (The Second Power) is one such system: it responds within about 2 milliseconds (small/medium capacity) to 4 milliseconds (large capacity), with a 10-year storage service life.

This matters because voltage sags are the most common power-quality disturbance in industry. According to IEEE-cited data, a typical industrial facility experiences roughly 60–70 voltage sag events per year, and in semiconductor fabs a single sag can scrap a wafer lot worth from tens of thousands to millions of dollars. The U.S. Electric Power Research Institute (EPRI) estimates poor power quality costs U.S. businesses between $119 billion and $188 billion annually.

Battery-free vs. battery-based protection

Conventional UPS systems store energy in lead-acid or lithium batteries, which degrade, require replacement every few years, and add maintenance and disposal costs. A battery-free approach uses EDLC ultra-capacitors instead:

  • Response: ~2 ms (small/medium), ~4 ms (large) — restoring voltage before most equipment faults.
  • Storage life: ~10 years, with no periodic battery swaps.
  • Scope: low-voltage (LV) loads — semiconductor, display, automotive, and battery manufacturing lines.
